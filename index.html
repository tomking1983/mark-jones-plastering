Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the difference between plastering and rendering?

    Plastering is the process of applying a thin layer of material over a wall or surface to provide a smooth finish. Rendering is a more specialized technique that involves applying a thicker layer of cement-based mix, usually reinforced with a mesh, to provide a more durable, waterproof surface.

  • Is plastering or rendering suitable for outdoor use?

    Yes, both plastering and rendering can be used outdoors, but it is important to ensure that the surface is properly prepared and protected from the elements before applying the material.

  • How long does it take for plastering or rendering to dry?

    The drying time for plastering or rendering will depend on the ambient temperature and humidity, as well as the type and thickness of the material used. Generally, it can take anywhere from several hours to several days for the material to dry completely.

  • What are the common causes of cracking in plastering or rendering?

    Cracking in plastering or rendering can be caused by a variety of factors, such as poor preparation of the surface, incorrect mixing of the material, uneven application, and rapid drying.

  • How often should plastering or rendering be re-applied?

    The frequency at which plastering or rendering should be re-applied will depend on the conditions the surface is exposed to, as well as the type of material used. As a general rule, it is recommended to re-apply the material every 2-3 years.
